Welcome to the journey

In the world of cybersecurity, vulnerabilities don't exist in a vacuum. They are exploitable because of how systems are connected, configured, and communicate at a fundamental network level. This blog is dedicated to that principle.

I'm a certified professional with a passion for all things cyber, holding certifications in networking (CCNA), foundational security (CompTIA Security+), and practical penetration testing (PNPT). I'm currently deepening my expertise by tackling the Certified Penetration Testing Specialist (CPTS) from Hack The Box.

This blog is my playbook, my lab journal, and my contribution to the community. My goal is to move beyond generic tutorials and establish a unique expert persona: The Network-Aware Pentester. Here, we'll explore how a deep understanding of the network stack is the ultimate weapon in an ethical hacker's arsenal.

What to Expect: The Core Vectors

Vector 1: The Professional Pentesting Playbook

  • Real-World Engagement Walkthroughs: Multi-part series simulating professional engagements, from OSINT to domain compromise and reporting.
  • Active Directory Domination: Deep dives into AD attacks like Kerberoasting, with a defender's perspective.
  • The Art of the Report: Learn to communicate risk and write impactful findings.

Vector 2: Hacking the Network Stack

  • Exploiting Layer 2/3: Dive into VLAN hopping, ARP spoofing, and OSPF attacks.
  • Pivoting and Lateral Movement Masterclass: Techniques from SSH tunneling to Ligolo-ng.
  • Firewall & IDS/IPS Evasion: Learn firewall theory and bypass techniques.

Vector 3: The Certification Journey & Homelab Chronicles

  • Deconstructing CPTS: A detailed learning log of the CPTS certification process.
  • Building the Ultimate Pentesting Lab: Guides for building segmented home labs with pfSense and AD domains.
  • Certification Reviews: Compare PNPT, CPTS, and other certifications.

Vector 4: Tool-Craft & Automation

  • Building Simple Security Tools with Python: Tutorials for scripting custom scanners and recon tools.
  • Customizing and Chaining Open-Source Tooling: Combine tools like Nmap, httpx, and Amass.
  • Scripting for Recon and Post-Exploitation: Automate parsing and enumeration workflows.

Who is this for?

  • Aspiring and Junior Penetration Testers: Structured, methodology-driven learning paths.
  • Network Engineers and System Administrators: Blue teamers learning the offensive mindset.
  • Mid-Level Security Professionals: Deep dives into network-layer and AD exploitation.
